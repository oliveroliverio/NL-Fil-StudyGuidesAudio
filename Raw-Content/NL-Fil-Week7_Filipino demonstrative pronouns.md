**ChatGPT:**

The Filipino word **“panghalip”** translates to **“pronoun”** in English.
✅ **Breakdown:**

* **pang-** → a prefix meaning “used for” or “for the purpose of”
* **halip** → means “substitute” or “replacement”


So literally, **panghalip** means **“used as a substitute”** — which makes sense because pronouns are words that **substitute for nouns** (like *he, she, it, they*).
Example:

Si Maria ay maganda. **Siya** ay mabait din.
→ Maria is beautiful. **She** is also kind.

(*“Siya” is a panghalip that replaces “Si Maria.”*)

**ChatGPT:**

The English word **“here”** translates to **“dito”** or **“narito”** in Filipino, depending on the context:

| Filipino | English | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Dito** | here | Common, everyday speech → “Come here” = *Halika **dito***. |
| **Narito** | here / here is | More formal or emphatic → “Here is your book” = *Narito ang libro mo.* |


✅ **Summary:**

* *dito* → casual, locational (“in this place”)
* *narito* → formal, presenting something (“here is...”)
